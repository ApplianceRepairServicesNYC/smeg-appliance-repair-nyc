Common Problems and Professional Solutions

No Heat Production

When your Smeg microwave runs but fails to heat, the magnetron—the component generating microwave energy—has likely failed. Other causes include high-voltage diode failures, capacitor problems, or transformer issues. Our technicians safely diagnose high-voltage circuits using specialized equipment, then replace failed components restoring full heating capability.

Turntable Malfunctions

Turntables that don't rotate cause uneven cooking and hot spots. Problems typically stem from worn drive couplers, failed turntable motors, or damaged roller guides. We inspect all turntable components, replace worn parts, and verify smooth rotation before completing repairs.

Sparking or Arcing

Sparks inside the microwave cavity indicate serious problems requiring immediate attention. Common causes include damaged waveguide covers, deteriorated interior paint exposing bare metal, or metal fragments on the turntable or walls. Our technicians identify and address spark sources, replacing waveguide covers and repairing interior surfaces to restore safe operation.

Door Latch and Safety Switch Issues

Microwaves won't operate unless door switches confirm secure closure. Failed switches, misaligned latches, or damaged door seals prevent operation or allow unsafe running with doors ajar. We replace faulty switches, adjust latch mechanisms, and verify all safety interlocks function correctly.

Control Panel Problems

Unresponsive touchpads, dim displays, or erratic button behavior indicate membrane switch failures, control board problems, or display module issues. Our technicians diagnose whether problems originate in user interface components or main control boards, then implement targeted repairs restoring full functionality.

Convection Fan Failures

Convection microwaves depend on fan circulation for even browning and baking. Noisy, slow, or non-functioning fans compromise convection performance. We replace worn motors, damaged blades, and failed bearings, restoring the airflow essential for proper convection operation.
